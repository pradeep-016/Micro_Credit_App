How It Works?

Microcredit loans are small-scale financial aids, often given to people with limited access to regular banks. They're usually provided by microfinance organizations, and they help individuals, especially those with low incomes, start small businesses or invest in income-generating activities. These loans have flexible repayment terms and often involve borrowers forming groups to support each other. The goal is to empower individuals and communities, reducing poverty and promoting economic growth in underserved areas.
